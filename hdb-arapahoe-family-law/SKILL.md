# hdb:arapahoe-family-law

## Usage

```
/hdb:arapahoe-family-law <family-law document to draft or revise>
```

## Description

Prepare Colorado family-law documents for a self-represented litigant in Arapahoe County, including motions to modify parenting time, modify child support, appoint a Child and Family Investigator, motions to compel/enforce parenting orders, petitions for review of magistrate orders, proposed orders, certificates of service, and related post-decree domestic-relations filings.

## Overview

Draft practical, court-ready Colorado domestic-relations filings for a pro se litigant, with an emphasis on Arapahoe County post-decree parenting-time and child-support disputes. This skill helps organize facts, select current Colorado Judicial Branch forms, state the governing legal standard, and produce a clear motion or proposed order without giving legal advice.

## Non-Negotiables

- Say that the user is self-represented/pro se only as a filing status, not as an excuse for weak drafting.
- Do not invent facts, dates, communications, income figures, overnights, or conferral history. Use placeholders like `[date]`, `[amount]`, or `[describe exhibit]` when information is missing.
- Preserve party titles from the original case. The person filing a post-decree motion does not become "Petitioner" merely because they are filing the motion.
- Check Arapahoe County Division Practice Standards for page limits (typically 15 pages), exhibit tagging (Petitioner: Numbers; Respondent: Letters), and mandatory mediation requirements.
- Require a detailed conferral statement. A single last-minute email is insufficient. Document multiple attempts and methods to demonstrate a "Good Faith Effort" per C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15.
- Prefer current official Colorado Judicial Branch forms when they exist. Use a custom motion only when the user asks for a narrative filing, needs an attachment/memorandum, or the official form is insufficient.
- Verify current forms, statutes, rules, and local requirements online when the filing date matters. Colorado family-law forms and child-support calculations changed in 2026.
- Avoid unnecessary personally identifying information about children. Use initials or "the minor child" unless the form, caption, or facts require full names.
- Include a verification only when the filing should be verified or the user asks for one. Use Colorado penalty-of-perjury language if included.
- Include a certificate of service. Distinguish between "Service via Colorado Courts E-Filing (CCE)" for registered parties and "Service via US Mail" for others.

## Drafting Workflow

1. Identify the document type: motion, response (usually due 21 days after motion filing per C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15), reply, petition for review, proposed order, declaration/verification, or certificate of service.
2. Gather the filing frame:
   - Court, county, address, case number, division/courtroom. For Arapahoe County DR matters, the usual court address is `7325 S. Potomac Street, Centennial, Colorado 80112`; verify against the current order or docket.
   - Original party titles and current filing party.
   - Current operative orders by date and title.
   - Requested relief (or specific points of opposition for a Response).
   - Detailed conferral history: dates, methods, and specific points of agreement/disagreement to demonstrate a "Good Faith Effort."
   - Evidence/exhibits available (verify tagging per Division Practice Standards).
   - Deadlines, hearing dates, or time-sensitive events.
3. Select the right vehicle:
   - Parenting-time modification: start with JDF 1406 and proposed order JDF 1424; attach or draft a proposed Parenting Plan JDF 1113 if useful.
   - Child-support modification: use HB25-1159 compliant 2026 worksheets.
   - CFI appointment: use JDF 1317 and proposed order JDF 1318; address scope, reasons appointment is needed, and fee allocation.
   - Parenting-time enforcement/disputes: consider JDF 1418 under C.R.S. § 14-10-129.5 when enforcing an existing order rather than changing it.
   - Magistrate review: draft a petition focused on preserved errors, standard of review (clearly erroneous for facts; de novo for law), and timeliness.
4. Draft the filing in a restrained litigation voice: specific, chronological, factual, and child-focused. Avoid insults, speculation about motives unless tied to admissible facts, and broad accusations that are not necessary to the relief.
5. End with exact relief the court can order. A proposed order should be administrable without further interpretation.

## Document Structure

Use this structure for custom motions or responses unless a JDF form controls:

```markdown
| DISTRICT COURT, ARAPAHOE COUNTY, COLORADO<br />Court Address: 7325 S. Potomac Street<br />Centennial, Colorado 80112 | |
| :-- | :-- |
| In re the Marriage of / Parental Responsibilities concerning:<br />Petitioner: [name]<br />Respondent/Co-Petitioner: [name] | |
| Filed by:<br />[name], pro se<br />[address]<br />Phone: [phone]<br />Email: [email] | Case Number: [case number]<br />Division: [division] |
| [DOCUMENT TITLE IN CAPS, e.g., RESPONSE TO MOTION TO MODIFY] | |
```

For petitions or motions concerning a magistrate order, keep the same case caption and Arapahoe County court address unless the docket shows the case was transferred. "Magistrate" is the judicial officer; identify them as "Magistrate [Name]" to ensure the correct standard of review is applied. If the docket or order identifies Jefferson County Combined Court, the address is `100 Jefferson County Parkway, Golden, CO 80401`. Be alert for bad copied captions from older drafts that list Jefferson County, Golden, or Denver when the case number is an Arapahoe County domestic-relations case.

Then use these sections as appropriate:

- `Certificate of Compliance re: C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15`: A detailed statement of the "Good Faith Effort" to confer, including dates and methods.
- `Background/Facts` with numbered paragraphs.
- `Legal Standard` with current statutory authority (referencing HB25-1159 for 2026 support cases).
- `Argument` organized by issue, not emotion.
- `Requested Relief` with numbered or lettered orders.
- `Verification`, if needed.
- `Certificate of Service`.

For proposed orders, use a shorter structure:

```markdown
PROPOSED ORDER RE: [MOTION TITLE]

The Court, having reviewed [motion title], any response, the file, and applicable law, finds:

1. [Jurisdiction/procedural finding.]
2. [Good-cause/best-interests/statutory finding.]

IT IS ORDERED:

1. [Specific order.]
2. [Deadline/method/compliance detail.]
3. [Reservation/denial of remaining relief if appropriate.]

Date: ____________        BY THE COURT: ______________________
```

## Motion-Specific Guidance

### Modification of Parenting Time

Anchor the request in C.R.S. § 14-10-129 and the best-interests factors in C.R.S. § 14-10-124. Separate three concepts:

- A schedule adjustment that serves best interests.
- A change to the parent with whom the child resides a majority of the time, which can trigger timing limits and a higher showing.
- A restriction/supervision request, which requires danger/endangerment allegations and special care.

Draft facts around the current order, how the schedule has actually operated, the children's adjustment, school/activity logistics, each parent's ability to support the children's relationship with the other parent, and the concrete proposed schedule. Note if mediation has already occurred as required by many Arapahoe County divisions.

### Modification of Child Support

Anchor the request in C.R.S. §§ 14-10-122 and 14-10-115. Use 2026 HB25-1159 criteria:
- Explain the substantial and continuing change.
- Address the updated Self-Support Reserve (SSR) if applicable.
- Calculate "Direct Expenses" under the new shared-parenting rules.
- State whether the requested effective date is the filing date or another legally supportable date. Use current 2026 calculator/form terminology; do not rely on old Worksheet A/B or 93-overnight language unless drafting about a pre-2026 order or verified current forms require it.

### Appointment of a CFI

Anchor the request in C.R.S. § 14-10-116.5, CJD 04-08, CJD 04-05 when state payment is requested, and JDF 1317/JDF 1318. Explain why neutral investigation would help the court decide parenting time, decision-making, relocation, safety, school/therapy, or communication issues. Propose a narrow scope, identify records/interviews needed, state whether the parties agree on any CFI candidates, and propose fee allocation or state-payment paperwork if relevant.

### Motions to Compel or Enforce

Use a narrow problem/solution format: existing right or order, specific request made, documented conferral (multiple attempts), noncompliance/refusal, prejudice to the child or parent, and precise relief. For child records, consider C.R.S. § 14-10-123.8. For missed or interfered parenting time, consider C.R.S. § 14-10-129.5 and JDF 1418.

### Petition for Review of Magistrate Order

Focus on timeliness (typically 14 or 21 days), the order being reviewed, issues presented, and the standard of review (Findings of Fact are reviewed for "Clear Error" per CRM 7; Conclusions of Law are reviewed "De Novo"). Avoid rearguing every grievance; pick the errors that matter. Identify the officer specifically as "Magistrate [Name]."

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, confirm:

- The caption court and county are correct. Arapahoe County District Court is commonly listed at `7325 S. Potomac Street, Centennial, Colorado 80112`; verify if using a different courthouse or case type.
- Party titles match the original case.
- Every factual assertion has a source, exhibit, message, order, or firsthand basis.
- The requested relief is specific enough for immediate entry as an order.
- Any statutory citations are current as of the filing date.
- The document includes conferral, verification, signature block, and service certificate when applicable.
- The draft distinguishes legal information from legal advice and flags points the user should confirm with an attorney or self-help center.
